Question 2 (15 marks)

Suppose we are modeling a simple linear regression, y is the independent variable and x is the dependent variable.

x<-c(1,3,4,7,8)
y<-c(12,11,7,5,3)
cbind(x,y)
##      x  y
## [1,] 1 12
## [2,] 3 11
## [3,] 4  7
## [4,] 7  5
## [5,] 8  3
  1. Write down the true model. (2 marks)

Answer: \(y=\beta_0+\beta_1x+\epsilon\) (2 marks)

  1. Write down the estimated model. (2 marks)

Answer: \(\hat{y}=\hat{\beta}_0+\hat{\beta}_1x\) or \(\hat{y}=b_0+b_1x\) (2 marks)

  1. Write down the residual. (2 marks)

Answer: \(e=y-\hat{y}\) or (\(\hat{\epsilon}=y-\hat{y}\) ) (2 marks)

  1. What is the assumption of the residual \(\epsilon\) (Hint: What is the distribution)? (2 marks)

Answer:

  1. We assume the \(\epsilon\) follows normal distribution with mean 0 and a constant standard deviation. (1 mark)
  2. The error terms are independent. (1 mark)
  1. Find the sample correlation coefficient \(r\) between \(x\) and \(y\), by the formula. (4 marks)

\[r=\frac{\sum(x-\bar{x})(y-\bar{y})}{\sqrt{\sum(x-\bar{x})^2\sum(y-\bar{y})^2}}=\frac{\sum(x-\bar{x})(y-\bar{y})}{(n-1)s_xs_y}\] (given \(\sum(x-\bar{x})(y-\bar{y})= -42.8\), \(s_x=2.88\), \(s_y=3.85\))

Answer:
